About

This site is for the PHI 182A course “Selected Philosophers: Socrates, Epicureans, Stoics” (Spring 2020) at Hofstra University.

In addition to this site, we will also use a Blackboard site. Blackboard is available through the password-protected Hofstra Portal. Why use both? This site is easy for you to access and for me to publish to, whereas the Blackboard site can present password-protected information. Material in copyright and grades will be available through Blackboard. There is a link to Blackboard at the top of this (and every) page ↑.

Structure of this site

All the site’s posts — topics, assignments, test questions, and similar — are available on the front page in chronological order. There are also shortcut buttons at the top if you want to focus on just posts about topics or tests.

  • Topics pages describe topics, which normally span several class sessions. They list Required reading assignments, with details about how to find them, as well as further, not-required content you may be interested in under See also. Afternotes, if any, refer to follow-up items from class, things students send me, and similar.

  • Under schedule is the day-by-day schedule in a stripped-down, minimal format with brief mentions of anything due or otherwise scheduled (readings, assignments, tests).

  • Tests pages gradually accumulate test questions for your first test (= “midterm exam”) and second test (= “final exam”). As there are two tests, there will be a list of possible questions for each one.

Share things

Think of anything that reminds you of what we’re talking about in the course? Students sometimes send me things others might enjoy seeing. It’s straightforward for me to add such things to the topics pages.
