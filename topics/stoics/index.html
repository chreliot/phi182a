Topic 3: Stoics

Like Socrates’ vision of Philosophy and Epicureanism, Stoicism was for its ancient advocates most of all a way of living. Coming to understand what is really good, they argued, and how the good and happiness are rooted in nature, could lead us toward perfect lives.

Readings

  1. Epictetus, The Handbook, text available on Blackboard > Course Documents.
  2. Seneca, from Letters on Ethics: To Lucilius (Univ. of Chicago Press), translated with an Introduction and Commentary by Margaret Graver and A. A. Long. Selections TBA, text available on Blackboard > Course Documents.
  3. Marcus Aurelius, from Meditations: A New Translation (Modern Library), translated by Gregory Hays.

After ejection I had about thirty seconds to make my last statement in freedom before I landed in the main street of a little village right ahead. And so help me, I whispered to myself: “Five years down there, at least. I’m leaving the world of technology and entering the world of Epictetus.”
